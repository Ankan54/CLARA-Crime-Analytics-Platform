BRIEF FACTS / COMPLAINT NARRATIVE
The complainant Shobha Krishnamurthy, aged 24, a Part-time Worker from Mangaluru, states that on 2026-06-08 they received a message on Telegram from a user named @tasks_support offering a 'simple online task job' with daily earnings of Rs. 2000-5000. The complainant was added to a Telegram group named Daily Earning Tasks and asked to complete simple tasks (liking videos, rating products) on an app called TaskMaster Pro. Initial small payments of Rs. 200-500 were received successfully, building trust. The complainant was then asked to 'upgrade to VIP membership' by depositing Rs. 5,000. After this, they were shown fake profits and asked to deposit increasingly larger sums to 'unlock withdrawals.' The complainant deposited a total of Rs. 94,965 across 4 transactions to UPI IDs dailywork003@fino, worker006@icici and account 4440988776655003. When the complainant attempted to withdraw, the app showed an error and the group admins stopped responding. Phone numbers 3538160812 used by the operators are now unreachable.

During the course of the investigation, a forensic analysis of the mobile application and communication logs was conducted. Technical analysis revealed that the fraudulent application was hosted on a server with the IP address 103.74.19.141. Further scrutiny of the beneficiary details provided by the bank revealed an additional linked account number 4440988776655006 utilized for layering the proceeds of the crime. Additionally, call detail records and WhatsApp metadata analysis identified another mobile number, 353816081234502, which was frequently active during the transaction period and is believed to belong to a key conspirator involved in the racket. Efforts are underway to trace the location of the suspects operating these accounts and digital footprints.

BRIEF FACTS / COMPLAINT NARRATIVE
The complainant Shivarudrappa Bhat, aged 18, a Engineering Student from Shivamogga, states that on 2026-06-12 they received a message on Telegram from a user named @tasks_support offering a 'simple online task job' with daily earnings of Rs. 2000-5000. The complainant was added to a Telegram group named Daily Earning Tasks and asked to complete simple tasks (liking videos, rating products) on an app called TaskMaster Pro. Initial small payments of Rs. 200-500 were received successfully, building trust. The complainant was then asked to 'upgrade to VIP membership' by depositing Rs. 5,000. After this, they were shown fake profits and asked to deposit increasingly larger sums to 'unlock withdrawals.' The complainant deposited a total of Rs. 23,967 across 6 transactions to UPI IDs dailywork003@fino, worker006@icici, quickjob004@oksbi and account 4440988776655003. When the complainant attempted to withdraw, the app showed an error and the group admins stopped responding. Phone numbers 3538160812 used by the operators are now unreachable.

During the course of the investigation, technical analysis of the communication devices and financial trails revealed additional identifiers linked to the fraudulent operation. Forensic data retrieved from the complainant’s device indicated that the scammers utilized a secondary bank account bearing the number **4440988776655006** to facilitate the transfer of illicit funds. Further tracing of the transaction history exposed another account, **4440988776655004**, which was actively used to siphon and launder the deposited amounts. Additionally, a detailed examination of the call logs and messaging records associated with the extortion attempts highlighted the use of another specific contact number, **353816081234501**, which was employed to coerce the complainant into making further deposits. Cyber analysis traced the operational backend of the fraudulent application and the phishing interface to the IP address **103.74.19.141**, confirming the server location utilized by the accused to host the fake task portal. The investigation is ongoing to identify the individuals operating these accounts and digital footprints.

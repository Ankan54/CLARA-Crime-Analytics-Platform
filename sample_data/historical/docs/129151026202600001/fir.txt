BRIEF FACTS / COMPLAINT NARRATIVE
On 05-03-2026, I, Meena Krishnamurthy, aged 70 years, a resident of Udupi and a practicing Chartered Accountant, wish to report a sophisticated cyber fraud. At approximately 10:30 AM, I received an incoming call from the mobile number **9886818568**. The caller, posing as an official representative of the "Narcotics Control Bureau," stated that a crucial **parcel interception** had occurred at the international customs checkpoint. He alleged that the parcel was addressed to my residence and contained two kilograms of **narcotic substances**, specifically pseudoephedrine, alongside illegal documents.

The caller claimed that my Aadhaar details had been recovered from the contraband and that I was liable for immediate prosecution under the NDPS Act. He warned that a police team was en route to my home to execute a warrant. Panic-stricken, I pleaded for a chance to clarify. The call was immediately transferred to a purported "Senior NCB Officer," Vikram Singh, who claimed to be heading the investigation.

This fake officer demanded I remain on a continuous WhatsApp video call to ensure I was not "tampering with evidence" or communicating with accomplices—a tactic referred to as "digital surveillance." Under this intense psychological duress, I was instructed that to avoid a twenty-year prison sentence, I must urgently obtain a "No Objection" **clearance certificate** to prove my innocence. I was ordered to transfer my savings to a specified government treasury account for verification purposes.

Fearing imminent arrest and social humiliation, I complied and transferred a sum of Rs. 593,825 to the beneficiary account number **BG32427096475**. The video call continued for hours until I realized the deception. I request that a case be registered against the caller and the account holder.

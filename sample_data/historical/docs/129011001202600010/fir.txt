BRIEF FACTS / COMPLAINT NARRATIVE
On 20th March 2026, at approximately 10:45 AM, I, Bhavana Gaonkar, aged 53 years, a retired engineer and resident of Chitradurga, received an automated voice call on my mobile number 9782890413. The caller alleged to be an official from the "Narcotics Control Bureau" (NCB) and claimed that a parcel addressed to me had been held by customs authorities in Mumbai. The caller stated that this **parcel interception** had yielded significant quantities of **narcotic substances**, including prohibited tablets and MDMA, and that my Aadhaar details were found linked to the package.

Panicking at the accusation, I was immediately transferred to a purported "NCB Superintendent," who identified himself as "Officer Vijay Sharma." He spoke in an aggressive and authoritative tone, warning me that a non-bailable warrant had already been issued for my involvement in an international drug racket. He ordered me to remain on a continuous WhatsApp video call to ensure I was not communicating with anyone else, effectively placing me under "digital custody."

While the video call remained active, the impersonator demanded that I transfer my savings to a specified government account to verify my financial track record and obtain a "clearance certificate" to clear my name from the investigation. Fearing immediate arrest and social stigma, I complied under extreme psychological duress. I transferred a total sum of Rs. 20,06,000 in two tranches to the beneficiary account number BG53872097477. The fraudster claimed the funds would be returned after verification. However, after the transaction, the demands continued, and I realized the deception. I request that a case be registered against the unknown accused for cheating and impersonation.
